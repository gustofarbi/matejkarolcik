I want to start off by saying I am deeply grateful to everybody who helped me on my way. 
Plenty of people did. I will leave out certain details in order to preserve the privacy of people involved.

The following lines are a kind of confession. Even though some people are not portrayed in the best light, I do not wish
to lay blame on anybody. I claim responsibility for all my mistakes.

I am now 32 years old, a proud dog owner and fiancé. I am proud to say I have friends and family.
None of them truly know who I am or how I feel though. This is on me since I have always been afraid to truly show myself.

I am an expert in hiding. Myself, my mistakes, addictions, tastes, opinions, kinks, you name it. You name it and I hide it :D.
Hiding is second nature to me. This has been so ever since I was young. When you are taught some emotions are not ok to feel (most notably anger),
it can quickly expand to others. Emotions are not static points, but live on a spectrum. When you forbid one, all others are affected as well.
This way one can forget who one is pretty fast. Anything you deem unacceptable, you hide. There is a learning process to this, however.
Certain things can accelerate it.

I grew up in a small Eastern European town. I had both my parents and a sister. We lived in a three-bedroom apartment.
I was the firstborn. My sister came a year later. "I had a normal childhood" was a fact I rarely questioned.
While growing up I seem to have been a pretty normal, quiet kid. I was afraid to be at kindergarten. One moment
I can remember quite vividly was when I was trying to convince my mom to let me stay at home. It got better, but was a pain
nonetheless. From what I remember I had one or very few friends whom I was pretty jealous of. I wanted them to play with me.
I don't have many memories from home. Once I played football with my father outside in a park. This was a one-time thing though.
From my other interactions with him, I remember few. None were too nice. This time for me can be characterized by one emotion - fear.
My father much preferred verbal abuse. Being yelled at whenever I did something out of the ordinary, called stupid, idiot
or threatened was my baseline. I still thought this was normal.

I am a quick learner and pain of any kind is a good teacher, so very soon I learned not to do the stuff he disliked.
There were times when I would prove to myself and him that he didn't have the upper hand. Those are memories I cherish. I stood up to him.
As a teenager I rebelled. I got bad grades at school, 3-4 on a scale of 1-5 with 1 being the best.
I am sure I was not stupid but authority became a burden to me. Learning what they were teaching at school
was a weakness. I would rather fail at it. At school where there was trouble, I was certainly involved.
Of course whenever I did something good, it was criticized anyway. Or too easy anyway. No accomplishment was good enough.
I started internalizing that voice. Or maybe it was already there at this moment. I was shy, especially around women I liked.

At this time my father made it abundantly clear to me that we were not friends. He told me so himself once.
At Christmas one time, I got a deodorant as a present. My father liked the scent of it, and so he took it.
A lesson was learned that day. If you like something, and it belongs to you, it can be taken away at any time, and you
have to let them do it . Also, your 'no' does not count. A people pleaser in the making.

I was not an angel though. I stole some money from him, maybe 100 euro all in all. I would exchange them for Slovak crowns
and would buy myself and my classmates sweets. At least I was popular, right? I haven't felt any remorse for this.
An eye for an eye. The whole world can get blind. I feel some remorse now, but try not to be too hard on myself.
After all I was the kid and he the parent.

Sport was very important in our family. With my sister we swam when we were little. My sister persisted longer. 
I gave up after my first race. I lost and got no support, so I assumed this was the way with me. So I switched to judo.
The guys there were good at it but so was I. They were also bullies, not all of them, but they could sway the rest.
I could defend myself from one or two but not all of them. I don't remember if I told anybody, at this point I was
pretty used to help never coming anyway. So I just stopped going. The unwritten rule in our family was that you have 
to play some sport, any kind, but you have to do it. So I couldn't just stop, I took walks outside instead. The money
for judo I used for myself. At this point I was pretty terrified to appear at home when my mom was not there. She would
defend me before my father at least a little. This time it was winter and it got chilly. I would be roller-skating outside
in the cold when there was no snow, otherwise I would just walk.

Of course, I would get punished each time for each deed I did. Bad grades, stolen money, lying about going to judo practice.
But there is only so much one could take away from me. No computer, no TV. I was not going out much because I didn't
know how to relate to other kids. Instead, I would read. Books about Greek myths, Alexander the Great, Marco Polo were my favorite.
At some point the pain doesn't get much worse. You get used to it. You also get used to being treated that way.

I got a very distorted view of intimacy at home from my father, which deeply fueled my anxiety and led to an
early coping mechanism through porn. This was another thing I never questioned.
I am not blaming my father here, I believe this was truly his way of teaching me about the birds and bees.

I had a unique relationship with girls. At first, I was nervous, but tried to appear calm. Once I got warm around them,
I was charming, funny and smart. Not always of course, there was often some tension, mostly sexual.
After judo, I started lifting weights. My body got transformed, adolescent hormones helped. I was in shape and girls
noticed. I got quite popular, although I never could do anything about it. 
I know several girls had a crush on me, but by this time I had internalized the inner critic in my head fully. I knew for a
fact how disgusting I was as a person. Others were normal, not me. I had to be cautious not to show myself. Girls talk
and if they found out, I would be embarrassed. I did make some attempts but never could follow through.

My first sex was when I was 18. Despite what it seems to be, it is a good memory. This was a time of hope.
I would be leaving for uni soon. My destination was Munich, Germany. I got into the best school I could. I was the one
going abroad to study and felt proud of it. The inner voice was still loud but not as much in this period.
I was in a disco with some friends. The last summer before we would part ways. I had been working in Germany and had saved
some money. I don't know what time it was when I left. On my way home, there was this woman in a white dress. I didn't
know she was a prostitute. She asked me what time it was and I replied. Then she asked if I wanted a blowjob. I said no.
After a couple of steps I turned back and told her I didn't have much money. Only some coins. She didn't mind.
As far as sex goes this was pretty good, but I was no expert of course.
It was neither magical nor special. I was glad it was over with. This would be the last sex for some time.

Until this time, the only male role model I had, was my German teacher. Herr Nicolini would be the one who would tell me
I should not beat myself up if ever I made a mistake. He was kind, patient and smart. He was praising me often, although
I couldn't accept it. The voice in my head saw it differently. Herr Nicolini was old and I never met him outside the lessons.
I miss him and am ashamed I never visited him after I left Slovakia. This way I want to thank him for everything he has
done for me.

When I came to enroll at a university, I was almost paralyzed. I didn't know where to go, and I was too afraid to ask somebody.
Fortunately my aunt came with me with her at the time 1-year-old daughter. I don't know if I would have managed it myself.

While in Germany I finally met real male role models. The guys from my work truly saved me, I have zero doubt about that.
Seeing that older men can also be my friends was inspiring. None of them were perfect of course, but they admitted to their
errors and owned them. My personality began to change, although it was a very slow process. The patterns of my thinking
had been entrenched deeply inside of me.

I had been developing into a man. With my father mostly out of the picture it didn't get any worse.
I was still shy, fearful and without any boundaries. But things were getting better. My uncle and my aunt showed me
a family can function in a healthy and loving way. In a home where there is no fear and everybody feels safe.
I had to get used to this a little. Thank you M. and K. for showing me what a true family looks like.

My addictions had been developing for a long time, since adolescence. There was porn, of course.
As I was an adult now, alcohol came into the picture now. Not that I didn't drink before, but now I knew I could get alcohol
any time and could even drink it by myself. I didn't learn healthier ways of dealing with the anxiety and pain.

This was the time when I should've been fooling around with girls, but my sentiment was seemingly always: whatever,
I am not interested. I used to say that in order not to attempt and fail. This was safer. There were many times I had the
opportunity to sleep with somebody, but I couldn't allow that. When you hate yourself, you do not want to show that to
anybody. So I had to be drunk enough to override the voice in my head in order to do that.
This happened on a total of 4 occasions. Some of them nice, others not so much. Whenever I didn't
get drunk enough I would say I had to go home to sleep or that I didn't have a condom or that I was too tired.
Sometimes I would sober up a little in the bed next to a girl before sex, I would pretend to be sleepy then.
Of course, I would hide all of this.

At around 25 I went to the movies with my sister and R. We were watching the Baywatch movie. We used to get drunk before
the movie and this time was no different. I went overboard and got out of the theater before the movie ended. I can't
remember what happened after that. The gist is that I had a panic attack and was aggressive and wanted to fight the 
pope statue in Trnava. At this time I was under a lot of stress. Things were not going great after I graduated and I
struggled to find a new job. At the same time I had to pay off my university fees and pay the professor. So money was tight.
I couldn't talk about this to anybody, because in my mind admitting to a weakness was a weakness in itself.
I deeply apologize to anyone who had to witness that, especially my sister, Ra., Ro. and J.
I thank you for being there for me when I was in need of help and support.

At around the same time I started climbing with a girl. We became close friends and although there were attempts to move
it further, they never materialized. I lied about that and told some people we were together which was not true.
I was ashamed to never have had a girlfriend.

To Ro.: I don't know if I ever thanked you enough for being such a good friend. I consider myself lucky because I had
the opportunity to grow up with you. I am so sorry for punching you in Sastin, I should've asked you if there was anything you
wanted to talk about instead.

To K.: I never truly told you what I wanted to say. You were a great friend and I always enjoyed our talks.
I ended things too abruptly without any explanation and you didn't deserve that. I understand you were brought up
without the support of one of your parents, maybe both, and what I did was cruel and I regret it very much.

To M. and K.: thank you for showing me how a real family works. It was inspiring to witness your children grow up.
I never felt more at home than in Ebersberg with you. I hope you can forgive me for any troubles I caused you.

To my family: I love you and I want you to know you make me truly happy.
I do not always show it and hope to change that.

To my fiancée: you inspire me to be a better man. I would have never begun this journey without you. Watching you dance
is the biggest joy I ever had the chance to experience.
