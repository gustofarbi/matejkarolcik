I want to start off by saying I am deeply grateful to everybody who helped me on my way. 
Plenty of people did. I will leave out certain details in order preserve the privacy of people involved.

I am not 32 years old, a proud dog owner and fiancée. I am many things. A son, a brother, a friend, a coworker.
None of them truly know who I am or how I feel though. This is on me since I have always been afraid to trully show myself.

The following lines are a kind of confession. Even though some people are not portrayed in the best light, I do not wish
to lay blame on anybody. I claim responsibility for all my mistakes.

I am an expert in hiding. Myself, my mistakes, addictions, tastes, opinions, kinks, you name it. You name and I hide it :D.
Hiding is second nature to me. This has been so ever since I was young. When you are taught some emotions are not ok feel (most notably anger),
it can quickly expand to others. Emotions are not static points, but live on a spectrum. When you forbid one, all others are affected as well.
This way one can forget who one is pretty fast. Anything you deem unacceptable, you hide. There is a learning process to this, however.
Certain things can accelerate it.

I grew up in a small Eastern European town. I had both my parents and a sister. We lived in a three-bedroom apartment.
I was the firstborn. My sister came a year later. "I had a normal childhood" was a fact I rarely questioned.
While growing up I seem to have been pretty normal, quiet kid. I was afraid to be at kindergarden. One moment
I can remember quite vividly was when I was trying to convince my mom to let me stay at home. It got better, but was pain
nonetheless. From what I remember I had one or very few friends whom I was pretty jealous of. I wanted them to play with me.
I don't have many memories from home. Once I played football with my father outside in a park. This was a one-time thing though.
From my other interactions with I remember few. None were too nice. This time for me can be characterized by one emotion - fear.
My father used violance scarcely, but decidedly. He much prefered to use verbal abuse. I am not sure at which point it started,
but being called idiot was a daily routine. Being yelled at whenever I did something out of the ordinary was my baseline.
This one time I remember being woken up by his yelling and threats of a beating if I didn't come out of bed in an instant.
I still thought this was normal. Although the physical violence was not too often, he would make threats of it often.

I am a quick learner and pain of any kind is a good teacher, so very soon I learned not to do the stuff he disliked.
There were times when I would prove to myself and him that he doesn't have the upper hand. When he was putting us to bed,
he said he didn't want to hear peep, at which point I peeped. I got a beating in my bed. This is a memory I cherish. I stood up to him.
The only time we kinda got along was when he was drunk. He was a happy drunk but a drunk one. The next day a compensation
could be expected. As a teenager I rebelled. I got bad grades at school, 3-4 on a scale of 1-5 with 1 being the best.
I am sure I was not stupid but authority became a burden to me. Learning what they were teaching at school
was a weakness. I would rather fail at it. At school where there was trouble, I was certainly involved.
Of course whenever I did something good, it was criticized anyway. Or too easy anyway. No accomplishment was good enough.
I started internalizing that voice. Or maybe at was already there at this moment. I was shy, especially around women I liked.

At this time my father made it abundantly clear to me that we were not friends. He told me so himself once.
At Christmas one time, I got a deodorant as a present. It was in a set with a shower gel or shampoo.
This was a time when something like that was not as ordinary as now. My father liked the scent of it, and so he took.
A lesson was learned that day. If you like something, and it belongs to you, it can be taken away at any time and you
have to let it happen. Also, your 'no' does not count. A people pleaser in the making.
Another Christmas I got a fanny pack and liked it too. But so did my father. He took it again, and was wearing it for
a long time. Each time I saw it, it reminded me of my loss.

I was not an angel though. I stole some money from, maybe 100 euro all in all. I would exchange them for Slovak crowns
and would buy myself and my classmates sweets. At least I was popular, right? I haven't felt any remorse for this.
An eye for an eye. The whole world can get blind. I feel some remorse now, but try not to be too hard on myself.
After all I was the kid and he the parent.

Sport was very important in our family. With my sister we swam when we were little. My sister persisted longer. 
I gave up after my first race. I lost and got no support, so I assumed this was the way with me. So I switched to judo.
The guys there were good at it but so was I. They were also bullies, not all of them, but they could sway the rest.
I could defend myself from one or two but not all of them. I don't remember if I told anybody, at this point I was
pretty used to help never coming anyway. So I just stopped going. The unwritten rule in our family was that you have 
to play some sport, any kind, but you have to do it. So I couldn't just stop, I took walks outside instead. The money
for judo I used for myself. At this point I was pretty terrified to appear at home when my mom was not there. She would
defend me before my father at least a little. This time it was winter and it got chilly. I would be roller-skating outside
in the cold when there was no snow, otherwise I would just walk.

Of course, I would get punished each time for each deed I did. Bad grades, stolen money, lying about going to judo practice.
But there is only so much one could take away from me. No computer, no TV. I was not going out much because I didn't
know how relate to other kids. Instead, I would read. Books about greek myths, Alexander the great, Marco Polo were my favorite.
At some point the pain doesn't get much worse. You get used to it. You also get used to being treated that way.

We had sexual education at school as far as I can remember. But I also got a lesson at home. My father was an avid porn

I got out as soon as I could. I don't remember details about this, but the memory is there.
This is how my porn addiction began I guess. Of course, I couldn't express it, but I would use it to treat anxiety of 
which I had plenty. The more severe the anxiety, the more ugly the porn. This was another thing I never questioned.
I am not actually blaming my father for anything here, I believe this was trully his way of teaching me about the birds
and bees.

I had a unique relationship with girls. At first, I was nervous, but tried to appear calm. Once I got warm around them,

I know several girls had a crush on me, but by this time I internalized the inner critic in my head fully. I knew for a
fact how disgusting I was as a person. Others were normal, not me. I had to be cautious not to show myself. Girls talk

When I came to enroll at a university, I was paralyzed. I didn't know where to go, and I was too afraid to ask somebody.
Fortunately my aunt came with me with her at the time 1-year-old daughter. I don't know, if would have managed it myself.

While in Germany I finally met real male role models. The guys from my work truly saved me, I have zero doubt about that.
Seeing that older men can also be my friends was inspiring. None of them were perfect of course, but they admit to their
errors and own them. My personality began to change, although it was a very slow process. The patterns of my thinking
had been entrenched deeply inside of me.

I had been developing into a man. With my father mostly out of the picture it didn't get any worse.
I was still shy, fearful and without boundaries. But things were getting better. My uncle and my aunt showed me
a family can function in healthy and loving way. In a home where there is no fear and everybody feels safe.
I had to get used to this a little. Thank you M. and K. for showing me what a true family looks like.

My addictions had been developing for a long time, since adolescence. There was porn which I consumed almost every day.
